Situational Judgement Practice

Scenarios based on real Riyadh Air assessment questions. Choose the best answer, then check your reasoning.

Q1 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A passenger's seat recline is broken on a full flight. They are frustrated and raising their voice.
What is your BEST course of action?
A
Tell them no seats are available and return to duties.
B
Apologise sincerely, acknowledge frustration, offer comfort alternatives, and inform senior crew.
C
Ask the passenger to lower their voice first.
D
Move another passenger to free up their seat.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Empathy before solutions. Inform your senior crew. Small alternatives show initiative. Never tell a passenger to lower their voice — it escalates conflict.
Q2 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A vegetarian passenger received a meat dish despite making a special request during booking.
What do you do?
A
Apologise, check for alternatives, and offer what is available while noting the issue.
B
Tell them they should have confirmed at check-in.
C
Give them a vegetarian meal set aside for another passenger.
D
Apologise and say nothing can be done.
EXPLANATION
A is correct. Take ownership, apologise and actively find a solution. Never blame the passenger. Never take food intended for another passenger.
Q3 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
You feel dizzy and short of breath two hours into the flight. A passenger waves it off as 'probably nothing.'
A passenger reports feeling dizzy and short of breath. What is your priority action?
A
Give water and advise rest.
B
Immediately assess, notify senior crew and flight deck, check for medical professional onboard.
C
Wait 15 minutes to see if it passes.
D
Ask them to press the call button if it worsens.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Safety is always the first priority. Escalate immediately — dizziness and shortness of breath can indicate a serious medical event.
Q4 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A senior colleague tells you to skip the safety demonstration — 'no one watches anyway.'
What do you do?
A
Follow their lead — they have more experience.
B
Perform the safety demonstration as required. If pressured, escalate to the purser.
C
Do a shorter informal version as compromise.
D
Report the colleague after the flight.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Safety demonstrations are a legal requirement. No seniority overrides safety regulations. If pressured, escalate immediately to the purser.
Q5 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A visibly intoxicated and aggressive passenger attempts to board your flight during boarding.
What is the most appropriate action?
A
Let them board and monitor them.
B
Loudly refuse boarding and ask them to leave.
C
Immediately inform your senior crew and captain. Do not allow boarding until a decision is made.
D
Offer water and hope they calm down.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. Boarding refusal is a captain-level decision. Report immediately, act within your authority chain — never alone.

Emirates

Fly Better — Dubai's World-Class Airline

📍 Dubai, UAE 📅 Est. 1985 🖥️ Assessment Day (in-person) — full day, multiple elimination rounds

About Emirates

Emirates is one of the world's largest airlines, operating 200+ wide-body aircraft to 150+ destinations. Based in Dubai, it is renowned for luxury service, innovation, and its iconic A380 experience. Emirates recruits cabin crew from around the world and is one of the most competitive airlines to join.

Assessment Format

  • English Language Test (30 fill-in-the-blank questions, timed per question)
  • Personality Psychometric Test (180 questions, 45 mins)
  • Group Exercise / Discussion
  • 1-on-1 Final Interview

Core Values

Excellence in ServiceSafety Above AllInnovationDiversity & InclusionLuxury Experience

🔑 Key Tips

  • The English test is 30 fill-in-the-blank questions — all aviation-context sentences
  • Each question has its OWN timer (~30 seconds) — you cannot go back
  • The personality test has 180 questions — answer consistently with Emirates' values
  • Group exercises test teamwork AND leadership — show both
  • Dress code is strict: closed shoes, neat nails, no visible tattoos

Situational Judgement Practice

Scenarios based on real Emirates assessment questions. Choose the best answer, then check your reasoning.

Q1 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
During the group exercise on assessment day, one candidate dominates the entire conversation and dismisses your ideas.
What is the best approach?
A
Stay quiet and let them lead — avoid conflict.
B
Interrupt them loudly to make your point heard.
C
Calmly acknowledge their point, then contribute your idea clearly and confidently.
D
Complain to the assessor about their behaviour.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. Emirates wants crew who show both teamwork AND leadership. Asserting yourself calmly without conflict demonstrates exactly that balance.
Q2 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A First Class passenger complains that their meal is cold. You check and the galley oven was faulty.
What do you do?
A
Apologise and say the oven was broken — nothing can be done.
B
Apologise sincerely, explain there was a technical issue, offer an alternative meal or hot items, and notify your senior.
C
Quietly serve the cold meal — the passenger may not notice.
D
Blame the catering team.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Emirates' service standard is uncompromising. Own the problem, offer an alternative, involve your senior. Never make excuses or pass blame.
Q3 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A passenger insists on using their phone during final approach, despite repeated requests to switch it off.
What is the correct action?
A
Let it go — one phone won't crash the plane.
B
Firmly but politely explain it is a safety regulation and ask once more. If refused, notify the senior crew member immediately.
C
Confiscate the phone.
D
Announce it over the PA to embarrass them.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Safety regulations are non-negotiable. Always escalate non-compliance to senior crew — never take unilateral action like confiscation.
Q4 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
You are serving Business Class and realise you made an error — you served the wrong meal to a passenger who has a nut allergy noted in their file.
What is the immediate priority?
A
Wait and see if they react — they may not have eaten it yet.
B
Immediately inform the passenger, check if they consumed any of it, alert senior crew and access the medical kit.
C
Quietly replace the meal and say nothing.
D
Apologise and bring the correct meal.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. A known allergy error is a medical emergency in waiting. Immediately inform the passenger, check consumption, alert the team, and access the medical kit. Speed is everything.

SCENARIO

A

B is correct. The Emirates English test uses aviation-context fill-in-the-blank sentences. 'Overweight fee' is the correct industry term. Context clues (the weight being above the limit) point to 'overweight'.

Qatar Airways is the world's 5-star airline and has been named Skytrax Airline of the Year multiple times. Based at Hamad International Airport in Doha, it connects 160+ destinations.   • Video Interview (HireVue)

  • Math test includes currency conversion and time zone questions

Situational Judgement Practice

Scenarios based on real Qatar Airways assessment questions. Choose the best answer, then check your reasoning.

Q1 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
During the assessment day group exercise, the task is to rank survival items after a desert crash. The group is split and time is running out.
What is the best approach?
A
Stay silent and agree with the loudest person to avoid conflict.
B
Dominate the discussion to show leadership.
C
Actively contribute your reasoning, listen to others, and help the group reach a decision — even if not your preferred one.
D
Ask the assessors for more time.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. Qatar Airways group exercises specifically assess teamwork, communication and collaborative decision-making. Contribute clearly, listen actively, and help the group reach consensus.
Q2 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
A passenger approaches you upset — their special meal (diabetic) was not loaded on the flight.
What do you do?
A
Apologise and say there is nothing that can be done.
B
Apologise sincerely, check what food is available that suits their dietary needs, inform your senior, and monitor the passenger throughout the flight.
C
Offer them the standard meal and suggest they pick around unsuitable items.
D
Blame the catering company.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. A diabetic passenger without their special meal is a health concern, not just a service issue. Inform your senior, find appropriate alternatives, and monitor — this passenger needs special attention throughout.
Q3 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
Math test style — A passenger pays 500 QAR for a $110 item. 1 USD = 3.65 QAR. How much change do they receive?
How much change does the passenger receive in USD?
A
$15.07
B
$26.85
C
$27.40
D
$25.07
EXPLANATION
A is correct. 500 QAR ÷ 3.65 = $136.99. Change = $136.99 − $110 = $26.99 ≈ $27. Wait — recalculate: 500 ÷ 3.65 = 136.986. 136.986 − 110 = $26.99. Closest answer is A ($26.99 rounds to ≈ $27). Qatar's math test checks currency conversion and basic arithmetic — practise these.
Q4 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
You are mid-assessment group exercise. A candidate makes a factually incorrect statement that could lead the group to a wrong decision.
What do you do?
A
Say nothing — it is not your place to correct others.
B
Loudly correct them in front of the group.
C
Politely offer a different perspective: 'That's an interesting point — I was thinking about it slightly differently. Could we consider...'
D
Ask the assessors to intervene.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. This is exactly the kind of moment assessors watch for. Correct the direction without embarrassing anyone — show emotional intelligence and communication skill simultaneously.
Q5 / 5Situational Judgement
SCENARIO
Time zone question: Your flight departs Doha at 08:00 (UTC+3) and lands in London at 11:00 (UTC+0). How long is the flight?
What is the flight duration?
A
3 hours
B
8 hours
C
5 hours
D
6 hours
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Doha is UTC+3. Departure is 08:00 local = 05:00 UTC. London arrival is 11:00 UTC. 11:00 − 05:00 = 8 hours. Qatar's assessment tests time zone arithmetic — a real skill you use on the job.
