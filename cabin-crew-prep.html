Situational Judgement Practice

Scenarios based on real Riyadh Air assessment questions. Select the best answer, then check your reasoning.

Q1 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
A passenger's seat recline is broken on a completely full flight. They are raising their voice and visibly frustrated.
What is your BEST course of action?
A
Tell them no spare seats are available and return to your duties.
B
Apologise sincerely, acknowledge their frustration, confirm the issue, offer comfort alternatives (pillow, extra blanket), and inform your senior crew member.
C
Ask the passenger to lower their voice before you will help them.
D
Move another passenger to free up their seat without asking your senior.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Always empathise before you problem-solve. Informing your senior crew maintains the chain of command. Small comfort alternatives show genuine initiative. Telling a passenger to lower their voice escalates conflict — never do this.
Q2 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
A vegetarian passenger received a meat dish despite making a special request during booking. They are upset.
What do you do?
A
Apologise sincerely, check what alternatives are available, offer what you can, and note the issue for reporting.
B
Tell the passenger they should have reconfirmed their meal at check-in.
C
Give them the vegetarian meal set aside for another passenger who hasn't asked for it yet.
D
Apologise and explain there is nothing that can be done mid-flight.
EXPLANATION
A is correct. Take full ownership — never blame the passenger or the booking system. Actively search for a solution. Never reassign food meant for another passenger, as this creates a second complaint.
Q3 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
A passenger reports feeling dizzy and short of breath two hours into a long-haul flight. They say 'it's probably nothing.'
What is your immediate priority action?
A
Give them water and advise them to rest for a while.
B
Immediately assess their condition, notify senior crew and the flight deck, check for a medical professional onboard, and prepare the first aid kit.
C
Wait 15 minutes and check back — it may pass on its own.
D
Ask them to press the call button again if their condition gets worse.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Safety is always the absolute first priority — never delay on medical symptoms. Dizziness and shortness of breath can signal a serious cardiac or respiratory event. Immediate escalation to the flight deck and senior crew is mandatory.
Q4 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
A senior colleague tells you to skip the safety demonstration before takeoff. 'No one watches it anyway,' they say.
What do you do?
A
Follow their lead — they are more experienced and know the passengers.
B
Perform the full safety demonstration as required by regulation. If pressured further, escalate to the purser immediately.
C
Do a shortened, informal version as a reasonable compromise.
D
Perform the demonstration but report your colleague to management after the flight.
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Safety demonstrations are a legal and regulatory requirement — non-negotiable regardless of seniority. If a colleague pressures you to violate safety regulations, escalate to the purser immediately. This tests your integrity under peer pressure.
Q5 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
During boarding, a visibly intoxicated passenger is aggressive toward the gate staff and is attempting to board your flight.
What is the most appropriate action?
A
Allow boarding and monitor them closely throughout the flight.
B
Loudly refuse boarding and ask them to leave the gate area.
C
Immediately inform your senior crew and captain. Do not allow boarding until a decision is made at captain level.
D
Offer them water and a seat to calm down before the decision is made.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. Denying boarding is always a captain-level decision — never yours alone to make. Report immediately to senior crew and let the chain of command handle it. An intoxicated, aggressive passenger is a genuine safety risk to everyone onboard.

Emirates

Fly Better · Dubai's World-Class Airline

📍 Dubai International Airport (DXB) 📅 Est. 1985 🖥️ Assessment Day (in-person, Dubai or global open days) · Full day, multiple elimination rounds

About Emirates

Emirates is one of the world's largest airlines, operating 200+ aircraft to 150+ destinations from its Dubai hub. Renowned for luxury service, the A380 experience, and its iconic red uniform, Emirates is one of the most competitive and desirable cabin crew employers on the planet. They recruit globally and are known for their multi-stage, high-elimination assessment process.

Assessment Format

  • English Language Test — 30 fill-in-the-blank questions, each with its OWN timer (~30 seconds each, no going back)
  • Personality Psychometric Test — 180 questions in 45 minutes (answer consistently with Emirates values)
  • Group Exercise — teamwork challenge observed by silent assessors
  • 1-on-1 Final Interview — behavioural and motivational questions

Core Values

Service ExcellenceSafety Above AllInnovationDiversity & InclusionDubai Pride

🔑 Insider Tips

  • The English test has 30 questions each with their OWN individual timer — you cannot go back to previous questions
  • The psychometric test has 180 questions: answer as your ideal self, consistently — no contradicting answers
  • During group exercises, show BOTH teamwork AND leadership — neither dominating nor staying silent
  • Strict grooming standards: no visible tattoos, closed shoes, neat nails — you are eliminated before you speak if you fail this
  • Research Dubai and Emirates' latest routes, aircraft and initiatives before your assessment day

Q2 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
A diabetic passenger informs you their special meal was not loaded on the flight. They look worried.
What do you do?
A
Apologise and explain special meals are catering's responsibility — nothing can be done mid-flight.

Q3 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
Math test: A passenger pays 500 QAR for a duty-free item priced at $120 USD. The exchange rate is 1 USD = 3.65 QAR.
How much change does the passenger receive in QAR?
A
72 QAR
B
61 QAR
C
62 QAR
D
500 QAR — no change, item costs exactly 500 QAR
EXPLANATION
A is correct. $120 × 3.65 = 438 QAR. Change = 500 − 438 = 62 QAR. Qatar's math test assesses currency conversion and basic arithmetic — skills you use on duty-free sales and foreign currency transactions daily. Practise these calculations until they are fast and automatic.
Q4 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
During the group exercise, a candidate makes a factually incorrect statement that is leading the group toward a wrong decision.
What do you do?
A
Say nothing — correcting others looks aggressive in a group setting.
B
Loudly correct them in front of the entire group to get the right answer.
C
Politely offer a different perspective: 'That's interesting — I was thinking about it slightly differently. Could we consider...'
D
Ask the assessors quietly to intervene and correct the error.
EXPLANATION
C is correct. This is a key moment assessors watch for. The ability to correct direction without embarrassing anyone demonstrates emotional intelligence, communication skill, and collaborative leadership simultaneously. This one move can set you apart from every other candidate in the room.
Q5 / 5Practice Question
SCENARIO
Time zone question: Your flight departs Doha at 08:00 local time (UTC+3) and lands in London at 13:00 local time (UTC+0). What is the actual flight duration?
How long is the flight?
A
5 hours
B
8 hours
C
10 hours
D
6 hours
EXPLANATION
B is correct. Doha departure: 08:00 local = 05:00 UTC. London arrival: 13:00 local = 13:00 UTC (UTC+0). Duration = 13:00 − 05:00 = 8 hours. This is a classic Qatar assessment time zone question. Always convert both times to UTC first, then subtract.
